TRANSISTOR TITANS

History

- Established: MHS 2011 -
The Marion Area Robotics Society is a high school club with 3 teams. Since 2011, MARS Robotics has grown thanks to Mark Wallace & Andrew Margherio starting from a small group of individuals in a small classroom setting. Later in 2015 a complete infrastructure overhaul at our local high school provided us a stem lab with more room. The stem lab is a designated area where students can work on their robots. Mentorship & Community Involvement is constantly growing, along with past students coming in and volunteering as alumni. MARS Robotics is a strong extracurricular activity at Marion High School that teaches students engineering, code, and good sportsmanship.

Community Outreach

MARS teams make a difference in the community of Marion, Illinois. Ways the teams have reached out include:

• Robotics Summer Camp at the Marion Carnegie Library, 2012-2016

• HackSI – Weekend long “hack-a-thon” – Team members volunteer, teaching others about robotics/engineering

• Robotics demonstration at VA Hospital

• Black Diamond Harley Davidson Trunk-or-Treat – robotics demo

• Angel Tree participant – made possible by team member donations

• Coach’s Boards – designed and built wire and controller managers (coach’s boards) to donate to rookie teams

• Mentor to FLL (First Lego League) team

• Prepare and donate goodie bags with emergency robotics “quick fixes” to hand out at competitions

• MHS Homecoming Parade – generating interest in robotics with float and mini WALL-E bots built by team
